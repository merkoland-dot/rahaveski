How this works. Editorial reviews are the primary signal — independent test labs (iRunFar, OutdoorGearLab, Switchback, CleverHiker, SectionHiker, Treeline Review, Wirecutter, Better Trail), magazines (TGO, T3, Backpacker, Outside Online, Outdoor Life, Live for the Outdoors, Outdoor Magazin DE, Bergzeit, Countryfile, Advnture, Scotsman, Outdoors Magic), and affiliate blogs (The Trek, GearJunkie, Summitborn, Backwoods Pursuit, Adventure Alan, Mountain Misadventures, BikeHikeSafari, TrailGroove). Each editorial source pill carries a tooltip with the reviewer's note when available; ★ marks an editor's-pick or award. Shop ratings (Amazon/REI/Decathlon/Alpkit/Bergfreunde/etc.) are a secondary signal, collapsed by default — toggle "Shop ratings: expanded" in the toolbar to show all inline, or click any individual ▸ to peek. Wilson 95% lower confidence bound shown alongside raw shop ratings (faint outer bar = raw, solid inner = Wilson) to penalize tiny samples.

Why editorial first. A 65-night structured test by a Treeline Review thru-hiker isn't comparable to 65 Amazon stars. Independent test labs purchase bags rather than receiving freebies, run controlled overnight tests across multiple seasons, and write up failure modes. Magazine reviews vary but at least involve a known reviewer. Shop ratings reflect buyer satisfaction at the price point they paid, often after a single use — useful but noisy. The architecture protects you from inverted incentives (a €50 Amazon bag with 4000 ratings can outrank a €600 Western Mountaineering bag with 200 ratings purely on review-count weight).

Caveats. Some popular budget bags (Vango, Snugpak, Naturehike, Coleman) are rarely covered by editorial review sites — they may show no editorial reviews despite hundreds of buyer ratings, which is a signal in itself. Naturehike's "800fp" claim is widely disputed by independent testers; treat as 650fp equivalent. Snugpak comfort ratings run optimistic. Recency decay not implemented (a 2019 review counts the same as 2026). European stock pill is best-effort; verify availability in your region.
